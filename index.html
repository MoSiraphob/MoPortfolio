Film - Sound Design Class Assignment

Assignment 1 - Video Ads

Description: This assignment explores how sound design creates an effective and engaging experience in video ads. I learn how audio guides viewer attention and builds excitement. The goal is to design a sound environment that's both pleasing to listen to and strategically helps the ad work, making it memorable for the audience.

Assignment 2 - Stock Video

Description: For this project, my focus is on enhancing generic stock video footage by designing sound that feels believable—even when the source of the sound doesn’t exist in reality. I aim to apply core sound design principles such as the sound sphere (a psychospatial model), the use of diegetic and non-diegetic elements. The goal is to craft a immersive soundscape that not only supports the visuals but also gives the video a clear narrative and emotional impact, making it more memorable for the audience.

Assignment 3 - Godzilla vs Mechagodzilla

Description: For this task is to create all the audio using only sound effects, with no music allowed. This includes designing three unique one-shot sound effect files for Mechagodzilla: its vocalization, body movements, and laser. Additionally, I am required to provide a written explanation detailing my creative ideas, the methods used, and the specific sound sources and modifiers for each designed sound.

Assignment 4 - Game (Relumine)

Description: I am focused on designing the complete soundscape for the game. This assignment requires me to create a diverse range of sound effects, including the "Wake up scene" audio and environmental "Ambience." I will also be designing detailed "Footsteps" with seven variations, sounds for "Lift" operations, and the distinct states of "Crystal" interactions, such as turning on, shining, and turning off. Furthermore, I am responsible for the "Machine Activate" sound, "Machine Rotate" with five variations, and subtle interactions like "Collectable Holder," "Paper pickup," and "Crystal mouse movement," all contributing to an immersive in-game experience.

Assignment 5 - Ghost of Tsushima (Final Project)

Description: This final project allows for the integration of dubbing and music as appropriate for the project type, though the primary focus and evaluation will be on the quality of the sound design itself. My goal is to utilize all the skills learned throughout the course to produce the best possible work. The evaluation will be based on criteria including Sound Details, Synchronization, Audio Visual Concept, and Mixing and Sound Quality.
